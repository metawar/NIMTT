Trustees and Governance

Leadership, oversight, and accountability

The Northern Ireland Music Therapy Trust is governed by a voluntary Board of Trustees who bring a broad range of professional expertise across healthcare, law, finance, and the arts. The Board is responsible for setting the strategic direction of the organisation, ensuring effective governance, and overseeing financial and operational performance.

Trustees work closely with the executive team to ensure services are delivered safely, ethically, and in line with the Trust’s mission and values. They are committed to transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement, ensuring that all activities serve the best interests of the individuals and communities who benefit from music therapy.

Dr Marion Gibson

President

Dr Marion Gibson is a past Chair and founding Board member of the Northern Ireland Music Therapy Trust, established in 1990 under Dr Michael Swallow. She became President in 2019 after over 25 years’ service, witnessing the Trust’s growth and impact.


Her career began in nursing before moving into social work, including frontline support during the Troubles. She later specialised in psychological trauma, working internationally and becoming an instructor with the International Critical Incident Stress Foundation. Before retiring, she was Manager and Clinical Director of Respond Training, supporting trauma practitioners.

Michael Nicholson

Board Member

Michael is a doctor with over 30 years’ experience in the NHS, working in Northern Ireland and Merseyside as a Consultant Psychiatrist, after training as a GP in Scotland. He has seen the benefits of music therapy across mental health, community care, and disability services.


A lifelong musician, he has sung with the Belfast Philharmonic and Royal Liverpool Philharmonic Choirs, and plays keyboards in the PM Big Band. He is committed to supporting NIMTT’s continued impact across Northern Ireland.

Fiona Cassidy

Board Member

Fiona is an experienced solicitor who spent many years in private practice, including as Senior Partner at Jones Cassidy Brett and later as a consultant with Lewis Silkin. She specialises in employment, discrimination, public administration, human rights, and charity law.


A music enthusiast, she plays piano and violin and previously sang in the Law Society choir. Fiona joined the Board in 2022 and values working with the dedicated team at Northern Ireland Music Therapy Trust.

Gary Lightbody

Ambassador

Gary Lightbody is a Northern Irish musician, best known as lead singer and guitarist of Snow Patrol, and founder of The Reindeer Section and Tired Pony.


Through the Lightbody Foundation, he supports charities across Northern Ireland, including the Northern Ireland Music Therapy Trust. His work focuses on helping underfunded organisations across sectors such as mental health, education, and music, with a commitment to supporting communities across all backgrounds.

Nicky Swallow

Ambassador

Nicky, originally from London and raised in Belfast, studied music at York University before performing as a violist in Florence, where she now lives. She manages a leading chamber music festival in southern Tuscany and has built a successful career as a travel journalist writing about Italy.


With music therapy rooted in her family, she remains closely connected to the Trust, continuing the legacy of her father, Dr Michael Swallow, through her involvement and support.

Gemma Swallow

Ambassador

Gemma, originally from Belfast, began her career at the Belfast Festival at Queen’s before moving into theatre production in Scotland and London. She has worked nationally and internationally with leading companies including Théâtre de Complicité, the National Theatre, Young Vic, and Stomp.


In 2013, she became Technical Director of the National Theatre of Scotland, where she champions women in backstage roles and leads on sustainability, supporting environmentally responsible theatre practices.

Emma McCabe

Ambassador

Emma's passion for music therapy comes from personal experience. During a prolonged period in hospital as a teenager, she used music to express her thoughts and emotions when words were difficult.


Through songwriting and singing, she found a powerful way to communicate and cope. Emma credits music therapy with helping her recovery and inspiring her path into nursing. Now an ambassador for Northern Ireland Music Therapy Trust, she is committed to supporting others on similar journeys.

Supporting people across Northern Ireland through compassionate, evidence-informed music therapy.
