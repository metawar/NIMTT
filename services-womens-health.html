Services

Women’s Health

Music therapy can support women’s health by creating a safe, compassionate space for emotional expression, reflection, connection, and wellbeing. It can be especially valuable during times of transition, stress, recovery, identity change, or when women are navigating complex emotional and physical experiences.

Enquire About This Service Learn More
Supportive, person-centred care Helping women feel heard, supported, and connected through music.

Supporting wellbeing, identity, and emotional health

Women’s health includes emotional, relational, and psychological experiences as well as physical ones. Music therapy can support women through challenge, change, recovery, and reflection in ways that feel safe, flexible, and deeply personal.

Emotional wellbeing Identity and self-expression Stress and overwhelm Recovery and adjustment Connection and support
How We Help

What this service can support

Emotional expression and wellbeing

Music therapy can help women process difficult feelings, find space for reflection, and support emotional wellbeing through challenging or transitional periods.

Stress, anxiety, and overwhelm

Sessions can support regulation, calm, and emotional release when life feels pressured, demanding, or emotionally heavy.

Identity and confidence

Music can help women reconnect with self-expression, agency, and a stronger sense of self.

Recovery and adjustment

Music therapy can provide support during periods of illness, recovery, hormonal change, or major life transition.

Connection and support

Shared therapeutic experiences can reduce isolation and support meaningful connection with others and with self.

Who It Is For

Support across health, community, and care settings

This service may support women experiencing emotional distress, recovery, life transition, chronic illness, stress, trauma, perinatal challenges, menopause-related change, or other experiences affecting mental and emotional wellbeing.

Sessions can be delivered in one-to-one or group settings, depending on the person’s needs, the goals of support, and the wider therapeutic context.

  • Women experiencing stress, anxiety, or emotional difficulty
  • Women adjusting to illness, recovery, or life transition
  • Women seeking safe, creative support around identity and wellbeing
  • Services wanting more relational, person-centred support options
In Practice

A space to feel heard, supported, and understood

Music therapy can offer women a place to pause, reflect, and reconnect with themselves. It can support confidence, self-expression, emotional release, and a greater sense of steadiness during times of change or difficulty.
