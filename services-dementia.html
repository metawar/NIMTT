Services

Dementia & Older Adult Care

Music therapy can support older adults and people living with dementia by creating meaningful moments of connection, comfort, communication, and emotional expression. It can help people reconnect with memories, relationships, and a sense of identity, while also supporting wellbeing in care and community settings.

Supporting dignity, connection, and quality of life

Dementia and later-life care involve more than clinical needs alone. Music therapy can help create moments of recognition, joy, comfort, and emotional connection that support both the individual and the people around them.

Memory and recognition Emotional wellbeing Communication Comfort and connection Person-centred care
How We Help

What this service can support

Memory and recognition

Familiar music can help evoke memories, support recognition, and create moments of meaningful connection for people living with dementia.

Emotional expression

Music can offer a safe way to express feelings, reduce distress, and support emotional wellbeing even when words become difficult.

Communication and interaction

Shared musical experiences can support communication, engagement, responsiveness, and connection with carers, family members, and staff.

Comfort and reassurance

Music therapy can help create calm, support routine, and provide reassurance in moments of confusion, anxiety, or agitation.

Who It Is For

Support across care, community, and residential settings

This service may support people living with dementia, older adults experiencing isolation, emotional distress, reduced communication, or changes in cognition, as well as the wider care environments around them.

Sessions can be delivered in residential care, nursing homes, hospitals, day services, community settings, and other places where older adults may benefit from relational, music-based therapeutic support.

  • People living with dementia or memory difficulties
  • Older adults experiencing loneliness, distress, or reduced engagement
  • Care settings seeking more meaningful person-centred support
  • Families and carers wanting more connection through shared experience
In Practice

Creating moments that feel familiar, comforting, and alive

A song, a melody, or a shared rhythm can sometimes reach places that spoken language cannot. Music therapy can help people reconnect with memory, emotion, identity, and relationship in ways that feel immediate, human, and deeply personal.
