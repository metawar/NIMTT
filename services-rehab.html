Services

Neurological & Physical Rehabilitation

Music therapy can support people recovering from neurological injury, illness, or physical change by helping with communication, movement, motivation, emotional wellbeing, and connection. It offers a flexible, person-centred way to support rehabilitation and quality of life.

Enquire About This Service Learn More
Movement Supporting timing, coordination, and purposeful physical engagement
Communication Helping people express, connect, and participate more fully
Motivation Encouraging confidence, persistence, and meaningful rehabilitation work
Overview

Supporting recovery in ways that feel human and meaningful

Rehabilitation is not only about physical recovery. It can also involve changes in identity, independence, confidence, routine, and emotional wellbeing. Music therapy can support both functional goals and the wider human experience of rehabilitation.

Sessions are shaped around the individual, their goals, and the wider context of care, helping rehabilitation feel more relational, motivating, and personally relevant.

How We Help

Areas this service can support

Speech and communication

Music therapy can support expression, interaction, and communication for people whose speech or language has been affected by illness, injury, or neurological change.

Movement and rhythm

Rhythm and structured musical interaction can support movement, timing, and physical engagement.

Motivation

Music can increase engagement and help people participate more actively in rehabilitation.

Emotional adjustment

Therapy can support people emotionally as they adapt to injury, illness, loss, or major change.

Identity and confidence

Music can help people reconnect with strengths, preferences, memory, and a sense of self.

Who It Is For

Flexible rehabilitation support across settings

This service may support people living with stroke, acquired brain injury, neurological conditions, degenerative illness, long-term physical disability, or other rehabilitation needs.

Sessions can take place in hospitals, rehabilitation services, community settings, residential care, and other environments where people benefit from therapeutic, music-based support.

  • People recovering from stroke or brain injury
  • Individuals living with neurological conditions
  • People adjusting to changes in mobility, speech, or independence
  • Settings seeking meaningful rehabilitation support alongside wider care
Our Approach

A clear pathway of support

01

Referral or enquiry

We begin by understanding the setting, the person, and the goals of rehabilitation support.

02

Assessment

We consider communication, mobility, emotional needs, strengths, and therapeutic fit.

03

Therapy delivery

Sessions are tailored to support movement, engagement, communication, and wellbeing.

04

Review

Progress is reviewed collaboratively so support remains meaningful and responsive.

In Practice

Rehabilitation that supports the whole person

Music therapy can complement wider rehabilitation by addressing not only movement and communication, but also confidence, identity, emotional adjustment, and motivation. This can make rehabilitation feel more relational, engaging, and personally meaningful.
