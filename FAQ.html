About Music Therapy

Answers to the most common questions about what music therapy is and how it works.

What is music therapy?

Music therapy is a clinical and evidence-informed form of therapy that uses music and the therapeutic relationship to support communication, emotional wellbeing, connection, confidence, and personal development.

Do I need to be musical to benefit from music therapy?

No. You do not need any musical skill or experience. Music therapy is not about performance or talent. It is about using music in a therapeutic way that meets the person where they are.

Who can music therapy support?

Music therapy can support children, young people, adults, and older adults across many settings and needs, including emotional wellbeing, communication, neurodiversity, rehabilitation, dementia care, and more.

How is music therapy different from a music lesson or music activity?

Music therapy has therapeutic aims rather than educational or performance goals. The focus is on the person’s needs, feelings, relationships, communication, and wellbeing, rather than learning an instrument or improving musical ability.

Accessing Support

Questions about referrals, who can enquire, and how support begins.

How do I make a referral or enquiry?

You can get in touch through the contact page to discuss your needs, ask questions, or explore whether music therapy may be the right fit for an individual, group, or setting.

Can parents or family members contact you directly?

Yes. Parents, carers, schools, organisations, and other professionals can all make an enquiry. We can then discuss the context and the most appropriate next step.

Do you only work with organisations, or can individuals access support too?

This depends on current service availability and the nature of the referral, but the best thing to do is contact us directly so we can talk through the situation properly.

How do you decide whether music therapy is appropriate?

We look at the person’s needs, the setting, the goals of support, and whether music therapy is likely to be a suitable and beneficial approach in that context.

Sessions & Delivery

Questions about how sessions are delivered and what people can expect.

Are sessions one-to-one or in groups?

Sessions can be delivered one-to-one or in groups, depending on the person’s needs, the goals of the work, and the setting in which support is being delivered.

Where do sessions take place?

Sessions may take place in schools, healthcare settings, care environments, community spaces, or other appropriate locations depending on the service and referral context.

How long does music therapy last?

This varies. Some people may benefit from shorter-term support, while others may need longer-term input. The length of therapy depends on needs, goals, and setting.

Do you provide online sessions?

This may depend on the type of support required and whether online delivery is suitable. Contact us directly and we can advise based on your circumstances.

Training & Workshops

Questions about staff development, workshops, and professional training.

Do you offer training for schools?

Yes. We offer training for schools to support understanding of music therapy, emotional wellbeing, communication, inclusive practice, and related topics.

Do you offer training for healthcare professionals?

Yes. We can provide training for healthcare professionals and teams, tailored to the needs of the service, staff group, and clinical context.

Can workshops or programmes be tailored to our setting?

Yes. Workshops and programmes can be adapted to suit your audience, aims, organisational setting, and the themes you want to focus on.

About the Organisation

Are your music therapists qualified?

Can I learn more about the people behind the organisation?
