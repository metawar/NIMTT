Who We Are

Northern Ireland Music Therapy Trust is a charity using music to improve health and wellbeing across Northern Ireland.

We work with children, young people, adults and older people facing a wide range of challenges. Through professional music therapy we support emotional wellbeing, communication, rehabilitation and quality of life.

Helping people through the power of music

Music therapy can support people experiencing mental health challenges, neurological conditions, dementia, learning differences, trauma and more. Our qualified therapists create safe, supportive spaces where individuals can express themselves, develop skills and improve wellbeing through music.

6000+ Therapy Hours Delivered
30+ Partner Organisations
1000+ People Supported

How we work

Our therapists work collaboratively with families, healthcare providers, schools and community organisations. Each therapy programme is tailored to the individual needs of the people we support.

🎵

Person-centred therapy

Every session is tailored to the individual, supporting communication, emotional wellbeing and personal development.

🤝

Collaborative partnerships

We work closely with schools, healthcare providers and community organisations to deliver meaningful support.

🌱

Positive outcomes

Music therapy can improve confidence, emotional expression, social interaction and quality of life.

Making a difference through music

For many people, music therapy provides a safe way to communicate feelings that can be difficult to express through words alone. Our therapists use instruments, singing, listening and creative expression to support personal growth and emotional wellbeing.

From supporting young people experiencing anxiety to helping individuals with dementia reconnect with memories and emotions, music therapy can have a profound impact.
