Neurodiversity & Learning Differences

Music therapy can support neurodivergent children, young people, and adults by creating a flexible, responsive space for communication, emotional regulation, confidence, and connection. It can be especially valuable for people who process, express, or relate to the world in different ways.

Enquire About This Service Learn More

Supporting communication, regulation, and connection

Neurodiversity is not something to be “fixed.” Music therapy offers an affirming, person-centred approach that supports people in ways that respect their strengths, sensory needs, communication preferences, and individuality.

How We Help

What this service can support

Communication and expression

Music can provide alternative ways to communicate, connect, and express thoughts, needs, and feelings without relying only on words.

Emotional regulation

Structured musical experiences can help support self-regulation, co-regulation, and a greater sense of safety and predictability.

Confidence and self-esteem

Music therapy can create opportunities for success, creativity, choice, and positive self-expression that build confidence over time.

Relationships and interaction

Shared musical experiences can support turn-taking, listening, joint attention, trust, and meaningful social connection.

Who It Is For

Responsive support across different settings

This service may support autistic people, people with ADHD, learning differences, sensory processing differences, communication differences, and others who benefit from flexible, creative therapeutic support.

Sessions can take place in schools, community services, family support settings, healthcare environments, and other spaces where a person may benefit from relational, music-based support.

  • Children and young people with communication differences
  • People who benefit from non-verbal or creative therapeutic approaches
  • Individuals needing support with regulation and connection
  • Settings seeking inclusive, neuro-affirming support
In Practice

Working with strengths, interests, and individuality

Music therapy allows support to be shaped around the person, rather than forcing the person to fit a rigid model. Sessions can use rhythm, repetition, improvisation, familiar songs, structure, and play to support engagement and development.

How support is delivered

01

Initial conversation

02

Assessment

03
