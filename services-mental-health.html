Services

Mental Health & Emotional Wellbeing

Music therapy can offer a safe, supportive space for people experiencing anxiety, depression, trauma, stress, emotional dysregulation, grief, and other mental health challenges. Through music, people can express what may be difficult to put into words, build coping strategies, and reconnect with themselves and others.

Enquire About This Service Learn More

Supporting emotional expression, regulation, and recovery

Mental health difficulties affect people in different ways. For some, it may feel hard to talk. For others, emotions may feel overwhelming, disconnected, or difficult to manage. Music therapy provides a structured, relational approach that can support reflection, safety, and emotional growth.

Emotional expression Stress reduction Trauma-informed support Confidence building Relationship and trust
How We Help

What this service can support

Anxiety and stress

Music therapy can help individuals manage anxious feelings, reduce stress, and develop healthier ways of processing and regulating emotion.

Low mood and depression

Who It Is For

Flexible support across different settings

This service can support children, young people, adults, and older adults in schools, community services, healthcare settings, residential care, and specialist mental health environments.

Sessions can be delivered one-to-one or in small groups, depending on the person’s needs, goals, and context. We work collaboratively with families, carers, schools, and other professionals where appropriate.

  • Children and young people experiencing emotional distress
  • Adults navigating anxiety, depression, or trauma
  • People who find verbal therapy difficult or inaccessible
  • Settings seeking creative, relational mental health support
In Practice

Creating space for safety, reflection, and change

In mental health work, the relationship matters. Music therapy can help people feel less alone, more understood, and more able to process difficult experiences at their own pace. It can support trust, routine, emotional regulation, and reconnection.

Our Approach

How support is delivered

01

Initial conversation

We begin by understanding the setting, the person’s needs, and the goals of support.

02

Assessment

We consider presentation, communication style, emotional needs, and therapeutic fit.

03

Therapy delivery

Sessions are tailored to the individual or group using structured, responsive music therapy practice.

04

Review and outcomes

Progress is reviewed collaboratively so support remains meaningful, safe, and effective.
